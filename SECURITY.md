# Security and Safety Policy

Project Thorondor is an experimental open-hardware engineering project. Reports may involve software security, electrical safety, mechanical hazards, structural risks, or unsafe documentation.

## Supported Versions

Thorondor is currently in pre-release prototype development. Only the newest files on the default branch and the latest published release, when one exists, are actively maintained.

Older prototype files may remain available for historical reference but should not be assumed safe, compatible, or supported.

## Reporting a Vulnerability or Safety Issue

Do not publicly disclose an issue that could reasonably create an immediate risk of injury, equipment damage, fire, electrical shock, structural failure, uncontrolled rotation, or unsafe pond operation.

Report sensitive issues privately to Sillmiron Studios LLC through the contact method listed on the maintainer's GitHub profile or future official project contact channel.

Please include:

- A clear description of the issue
- The affected part, file, version, or commit
- Conditions required to reproduce the problem
- Photographs, measurements, logs, or test data when available
- The potential consequences
- Any temporary mitigation you have identified

## Examples of Safety-Sensitive Reports

- Rotor, hub, shaft, or fastener failure that could eject parts
- Tower or mounting instability
- Missing guards around pinch points or reciprocating mechanisms
- Unsafe overspeed behavior
- Electrical shock, battery, wiring, or fire hazards
- Pressure buildup caused by blocked airline or failed relief systems
- Designs that could damage pond liners, entangle wildlife, or create a drowning hazard
- Instructions that omit critical assembly or operating precautions
- Firmware or telemetry vulnerabilities that could affect safe operation

## Response Process

Maintainers will:

1. Acknowledge the report when practical.
2. Evaluate severity and reproducibility.
3. Restrict or mark affected files when an immediate hazard exists.
4. Develop a correction, mitigation, or documentation warning.
5. Publish appropriate information after the risk has been reduced.

## Responsible Testing

Do not test Thorondor designs where failure could injure people, animals, or damage property. Use barriers, safe distances, protective equipment, and conservative operating conditions. Never stand in the rotor plane during testing.

## No Safety Certification

Project files are experimental and are not certified by a professional engineer, testing laboratory, electrical authority, or safety agency unless a specific document explicitly states otherwise.
